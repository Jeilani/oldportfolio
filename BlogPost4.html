Time's On My Side

Posted on June 8, 2019

"Okay, what if I just sit down and try to dedicate 30 minutes and whatever happens happens."

This a thought that ran through my mind in one of my early days of learning to become a programmer. One of those days that every bit of my body was resisting the acts of sitting still and coding.

This occured to me after a bit of internal back-and-forth between my lazy side and my determined side. But after that perfect idea popped up, I managed to sit down and try to focus afterall.

It was reasonable because it was an achieavable feat. At the time, my daily goal was to do four hours of coding a day and I would almost never accomplish it. Some days I wouldn't even do a single minute. The problem was how much of a climb that goal of four ours seemed in my head.

So I did a quick self-psychoanalysis and realized why the feat seemed so terrible.

Framing

There are many things that I can do for several hours that seem to make time ephemeral. I continued on this idea. Obviously, it's because I enjoy those things. But I realized I enjoy coding too. So it logically follows that learning how to program can become one of those things that make time fly, if I just look at it in the right way.

Then the thought in the beginning of this blog entry. Okay how about I forget this idea of 4 hours but rather just try to sit down for 30 minutes a day and whatever happens happens. Throw away this goal that makes the act so scary and just go with something simple and see where that takes me.

Things haven't been peaches and cream since but it's certainly a useful framing tool that has turned a lot of potentially wasted days into productive days.
